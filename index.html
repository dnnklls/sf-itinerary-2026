🦭 Sea Lions at Pier 39 · K-Dock Free

Head to the west side of Pier 39 — you'll hear them before you see them. March–April is good season with dozens to hundreds of adult males hauled out on K-Dock. The Marine Mammal Center panels nearby explain their story. Late afternoon light is great for photos.

🎰 Musée Mécanique · Pier 45 Free entry

One of the world's largest collections of antique coin-operated arcade machines — 1920s fortune tellers, hand-cranked peep shows, player pianos, old-school pinball. Entry is completely free; bring $5–10 in quarters to actually play them. Genuinely weird and charming — not a tourist trap.

Pier 45, Shed A Daily 10:00–20:00 Free entry · $0.25–$1 per game Allow 45 min

🏭 Ghirardelli Square · Waterfront Walk Free to stroll

Beautiful restored 1893 brick chocolate factory, 3 blocks from the hotel. Worth walking through for the architecture. Optional: hot cocoa at the Ghirardelli Chocolate Experience (~$5–6) or skip it and just enjoy the bay view from the square. The full walk Ghirardelli→Pier 39 along the waterfront is one of the best urban promenades in the US.

900 North Point St 3 blocks west of hotel Hot cocoa: ~$5–6 (optional)

📚 City Lights Bookstore Free to browse

Founded 1953. The epicentre of the Beat Generation literary scene — Lawrence Ferlinghetti's legendary independent bookstore. Three floors of eclectic, thoughtfully curated books; the basement is dedicated to poetry. A genuine cultural institution, not a tourist set-piece. Worth 20–30 minutes.

261 Columbus Ave Daily 10:00–22:00 Free to browse
🥃 Pre-dinner drink · Vesuvio Cafe $10–15/drink

Directly across Jack Kerouac Alley from City Lights. A bohemian bar open since 1948 — where Kerouac famously stayed and drank instead of catching the bus to Big Sur. Two floors of Beat-era memorabilia and stained glass. Very real, not performative. Perfect atmosphere for a jet-lag-shaking cocktail.

255 Columbus Ave Sun–Thu 11:00–01:00, Fri–Sat 11:00–02:00 Arrive ~17:00 for a quiet table
🦀 Dinner · Sotto Mare Oysteria Arrive at opening

The definitive SF cioppino — a massive bowl of Dungeness crab, shrimp, mussels, clams, squid, and penne in tomato-wine broth. Order "The Best Damn Crab Cioppino" (~$55, easily feeds two). Arrive right at 17:00 when they open — waits build to 45–60 min within an hour. One of the best seafood restaurants in the entire city.

552 Green St, North Beach Opens 17:00 daily Arrive at 17:00 sharp — no reservations for parties of 2 ~$35–45/person

☕ Breakfast · The Buena Vista Cafe $12–18/person

An SF institution since 1916, literally steps from the hotel at the cable car turnaround. The place that introduced Irish Coffee to the United States in 1952. Classic American breakfast — eggs, bacon, pancakes. Fast service, no fuss. Perfect pre-Alcatraz fuel.

2765 Hyde St (steps from hotel) Mon–Fri from 09:00 · Sat–Sun from 08:00 $12–18/person

⛴ Ferry from Pier 33 · Depart 08:40 Pre-booked

Arrive at Pier 33 by 08:10 — 30 min before departure. The first ferry is the best: dramatically quieter crowds and stunning morning light on the island. The 15-minute bay crossing with the city skyline and Bay Bridge behind you is a highlight in itself. Present your ticket at the gate.

Pier 33, Embarcadero Departs 08:40 · 15 min crossing Arrive Pier 33 by 08:10 10–12 min walk from hotel
🏛 Alcatraz Island · Cellhouse Audio Tour $47.95 adult

Award-winning audio tour narrated by actual former prisoners and guards who lived here. Walk Al Capone's cell (AZ-85), the 1962 Frank Morris escape route, solitary confinement in D-Block (Stop 16 — don't rush it), and the dining hall. After the tour, climb to the recreation yard for the best 360° view on the island: SF skyline, Bay Bridge, Golden Gate, Marin. Walk the island perimeter trail for views most visitors never see. Bring a jacket — the island runs 10–15°F colder than the city.

Total time on island: 2.5–3.5 hrs Audio tour included · 11 languages Jacket essential — it's cold and windy Return ferry: aim for ~11:30 back at Pier 33

🥪 Lunch · The Warming Hut · Crissy Field $8–14/person

Eat with the Golden Gate Bridge framed directly in front of you. Equator coffee, soups, sandwiches, and baked goods from local Bay Area makers. Sit outside on the picnic tables. This view is free and extraordinary.

983 Marine Dr (west end of Crissy Field) Mon–Thu 09:00–17:00 $8–14/person
🌊 Crissy Field Promenade → Fort Point Free

Walk the 1.75-mile Golden Gate Promenade westward from the Warming Hut. Restored tidal marsh and beach with the bridge growing larger with every step. At the end, walk under the Fort Point arch and look straight up at the bridge from directly below — a view available nowhere else. Fort Point interior is closed on Wednesdays, but the exterior and the arch itself are the main draw.

Golden Gate Promenade · 1.75 miles · ~35 min Fort Point: 201 Marine Dr · Exterior always open Interior closed Wed · Exterior is the best bit anyway
Uber across the Golden Gate to the Marin side · ~$12–15, 8 min · Best photo of the trip
📸 Battery Spencer · Best Golden Gate Viewpoint Free

Universally agreed to be the single best vantage for Golden Gate Bridge photography. You look directly down onto the north tower with the entire SF skyline behind it — the classic postcard shot. A short 0.25-mile trail from Conzelman Rd. It is windy and fully exposed — layers needed. Have Uber drop you at the Conzelman Rd parking lot.

Conzelman Rd, Marin Headlands Always open · Free Uber across bridge: ~$12–15 Spend 20–30 min Wind-exposed — bring your jacket

🏪 Ferry Building Marketplace · Thursday Market Free to browse

The Thursday outdoor farmers market runs 10:00–14:00 — perfect timing. Inside the 1898 Beaux-Arts terminal: Acme Bread (SF's best sourdough), Cowgirl Creamery (taste the Mt Tam triple cream — it's extraordinary), Blue Bottle Coffee (the original kiosk that launched the third-wave movement), Humphry Slocombe ice cream (try Secret Breakfast: bourbon + cornflake). Outside stalls: spring produce, oysters on the half shell, tamales, pastries.

🏮 Chinatown · Ross Alley · Stockton · Waverly Place Free to explore

Enter at the ceremonial Dragon Gate (Bush & Grant Ave). Walk Grant for atmosphere — red lanterns, souvenir shops. Then move to Stockton Street one block west for the real thing: whole roasted ducks in windows, live seafood on ice on the sidewalk, produce piled high. The Broadway–Sacramento stretch is most intense. Find Ross Alley for the Golden Gate Fortune Cookie Factory (free to watch, warm fresh bag ~$4–6 cash). Duck into Waverly Place for the painted temple balconies and Tin How Temple (free, donations appreciated).

Enter at Bush & Grant Ave (Dragon Gate) Ross Alley: 56 Ross Alley · Fortune Cookie Factory Waverly Place: painted balconies Tin How Temple: 125 Waverly Pl, 4th floor · Free Allow 1–1.5 hrs
🥟 Dim Sum · City View Restaurant $20–35/person

One of SF's most consistently praised dim sum restaurants — cart service, not an order form. Arrive at 11:00–11:30 to get seated quickly on a Thursday. Get the har gow, siu mai, egg tarts, and whatever the cart lady is most enthusiastic about. It's a communal, chaotic, joyful lunch.

662 Commercial St (edge of Chinatown) Mon–Fri 11:00–14:30 $20–35/person Arrive 11:00–11:30 to avoid wait
🧁 Golden Gate Bakery · Egg Custard Tarts ~$4/tart

A legendary SF institution. Warm, flaky, silky-custardy perfection. There's often a line — worth it. Grab a few before or after dim sum as dessert. Cash only. Opens ~10:00, closes when sold out.

1029 Grant Ave Cash only May sell out — go earlier rather than later

🛍 Union Square · Shopping Free to browse

SF's luxury retail hub. Nordstrom, Neiman Marcus (the SF flagship has a famous glass-domed rotunda — worth looking up), Saks Fifth Avenue, plus Gucci, LV, Burberry within 2–3 blocks. Detour to Maiden Lane — a pretty pedestrian alley off the square with an actual Frank Lloyd Wright building at #140 (now a gallery). Gump's (135 Post St) has sold SF luxury gifts since 1861, including Asian art and jade — genuinely unique to SF.

Geary & Powell anchor intersection 1–2 hrs browsing 140 Maiden Lane: Frank Lloyd Wright building · Free to enter Gump's: 135 Post St · Est. 1861

🎵 Haight-Ashbury · Amoeba Music + Vintage Shops Free to browse

Amoeba Music (1855 Haight) — one of the last great independent record megastores in America. Massive vinyl and CD selection. Worth 20–30 min even if you don't buy anything. Held Over (1543 Haight) — the best vintage clothing on the strip: well-curated actual vintage, strong 60s–80s pieces. Wasteland (1660 Haight) — larger, good for denim and leather. The neighbourhood retains real character — not a performance of 1967.

The six Victorian "Painted Ladies" on the 700 block of Steiner St (between Grove and Hayes) with the SF skyline behind them. A Fisherman's Wharf institution since 1965 — the real deal for SF seafood. Whole Dungeness crab, cioppino (the SF-style tomato-seafood stew), fresh grilled fish, sourdough bread. 🦜 Filbert Steps · Wild Parrots of Telegraph Hill Free

The famous flock of cherry-headed conures — wild parrots descended from escaped pets, now a beloved SF fixture. Most reliably seen in the trees along the Filbert Steps (especially the ficus, cherry, and loquat trees). You'll hear them first: loud tropical squawking unlike anything else in SF. Mid-morning 09:00–11:00 is the best window. The steps are steep, beautiful, and lined with private cottage gardens.

Filbert Steps, Telegraph Hill Best: 09:00–11:00 You'll hear them before you see them
🗼 Coit Tower · Murals + Observation Deck $10 adult (deck)

The ground-floor lobby is free — covered in vivid 1934 WPA murals of California life (farming, Depression-era streets, factories). Genuinely impressive art. The elevator to the observation deck is $10 and gives 360° views: Bay, Alcatraz, Golden Gate, Bay Bridge, downtown. Go at 10:00 opening for shortest queue and clearest morning views.

1 Telegraph Hill Blvd Opens 10:00 daily $10 adult (elevator) · Murals: free Allow 45–60 min total

Walk south along Bridgeway Boulevard from the ferry dock — galleries, boutiques, dramatic views back across the Bay to SF. Then walk north toward Waldo Point Harbor (10–15 min from dock) to see the floating houseboat community: hundreds of permanently moored houseboats, each one eccentrically unique. Very photogenic, very unlike anything in SF proper. Public waterfront path, completely free.

Practical Tips

Getting Around
  • Uber/Lyft: best for cross-city hops ($10–25)
  • F-line streetcar: waterfront → Ferry Building ($3)
  • Powell-Hyde cable car: hotel → downtown ($9)
  • MUNI #39 bus: Wharf → Coit Tower ($2.50)
  • Walking: Fisherman's Wharf & North Beach are flat
  • Clipper card: load at Walgreens or MUNI station
Money & Tipping
  • Restaurants: 20% is the minimum expectation
  • Cafe counter: $1–2 per drink at the tip prompt
  • Uber/Lyft: 15–20% (app defaults are fine)
  • Bills often include 4–5% SF Health Surcharge — tip on top
  • Cash only: Golden Gate Bakery + some market stalls
  • Bring $40–60 cash as backup; everything else: tap-to-pay
What to Wear
  • Daytime: 59–63°F (15–17°C)
  • Evening & waterfront: 50–55°F with wind chill
  • Layers: t-shirt + fleece + windproof/water-resistant jacket
  • Alcatraz: pack an extra layer — 10–15°F colder on island
  • Battery Spencer: fully exposed and windy — hat helps
  • Compact rain jacket = windbreaker = one item, always useful
Safety
  • Your area (Wharf, North Beach, Marina) is safe
  • Avoid: Tenderloin, SoMa 6th St, UN Plaza — none are on this itinerary
  • Pickpockets target Pier 39 tourist crowds — use front pocket
  • Do not leave anything visible in a rental car
  • After dark: stay on Columbus Ave and the lit waterfront strip
