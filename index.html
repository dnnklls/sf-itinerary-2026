Practical Info

Getting Around & Tips

Getting Around San Francisco ▼
  • Cable Cars: The Powell-Hyde line runs directly past your hotel — touristy but genuinely classic SF. US$9 per single ride.
  • F-Market Historic Streetcar: Runs along Market St, Embarcadero, and up to Fisherman's Wharf. Best way to reach the Ferry Building on Day 2.
  • Lyft / Uber: Budget US$12–18 for most cross-city trips. Usually 3–4 minutes from Fisherman's Wharf.
  • Walking: The Day 2 loop from Ferry Building to Coit Tower and back is almost entirely flat once you're off Telegraph Hill.
Safety Notes ▼
  • Fisherman's Wharf, North Beach, Chinatown, and the Ferry Building are all safe, well-trafficked areas day and evening.
  • The Tenderloin district (between Union Square and Civic Center) has open drug use — it's not on this itinerary and easy to avoid.
  • Keep bags in front and phones pocketed on public transport — petty theft does occur.
  • Car break-ins are very common in SF — never leave anything visible in a rental.
Weather & What to Wear ▼
  • Early April: 12–17°C with overcast mornings that often clear by afternoon.
  • Wind on the Golden Gate Bridge is reliably strong — pack a windproof layer, not just a cardigan.
  • SF's famous fog ("Karl") can roll in from the Pacific mid-afternoon, especially near the Wharf.
  • Comfortable walking shoes are essential — the Filbert Steps up to Coit Tower are steep.
  • A compact rain jacket takes up minimal space and is worth having.
What to Book Before You Land ▼
  • Fog Harbor Fish House (Day 1 dinner): OpenTable — reserve 19:30 for Wed 1 Apr for bay-view seating.
  • City View dim sum (Day 2 lunch, optional): Call +1 415-398-2838 to book 11:30 on Thu 2 Apr for sit-down trolley service.
Arrival day tip (Mar 31): Cortisol from the flight will carry you for a few hours after landing. Resist the urge to push through and explore — sleep by 14:00 to reset your circadian clock. A light meal, a shower, and a short walk to Ghirardelli Square is plenty for today.
